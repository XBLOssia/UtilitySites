Phishing alert!

You are receiving this email because the WPSD #1 technology department has been alerted to suspicious email activity. You may have recently received an email which could put your account and/or personal data at risk if handled improperly.

This email may appear to have come from someone else in the district, possibly even someone you frequently correspond with. It may look like a legitimate request to open a link to an attached document. It may be a request that appears to come from one of our district leaders' "personal" email addresses.

These are examples of a type of scam email we have dealt with in the past, which is more generally called "Phishing".

How can I tell if an email is a phishing attempt?

Phishing emails can be difficult to spot, but here are a few red flags:

Red flag: Dropbox links.
Red flag: Unexpected links, requests and/or attachments.
Red flag: Unprompted email from someone you don't often correspond with.
Red flag: The email address used doesn't match the sender's name.
Red flag: Hovering your mouse cursor over a link shows that the link will take you somewhere unexpected.
Red flag: Receiving email from someone outside of usual/reasonable hours (we're talking 3 AM).
Red flag: Anything having to do with gift cards - Scammers love gift cards.

If you are unsure whether or not an email is a phishing attempt, contact the tech department.
